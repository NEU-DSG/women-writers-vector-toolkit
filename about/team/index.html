Women Writers Vector Toolkit Team

The Women Writers Vector Toolkit was created by a group of faculty members, staff, and undergraduate and graduate students at Northeastern University. For a full list of these members, visit the team members page. The project has taken a handful of years to come to fruition, and in that time the project has morphed and changed. To incorporate the strengths of each team members into the project, we divided the team into three groups to work simultaneously throughout the project during Summer and Fall 2018.

Most of the members of this project worked in multiple team groups. This allowed for crossover of ideas, better communication, and smoother transitions with the workload as the project progressed. The Women Writers Vector Toolkit aimed to have a collaborative, inclusive, and enriching environment for its members as the project grew.

The Corpus Team included most, if not all, of the graduate students who worked in Summer and Fall 2018. This group focused on the model creation, testing, and application side of the WWVT.

The Infrastructure Team worked on the creation and upkeep of the original version of the Word Vector Interface held on a Shiny App. The infrastructure team then built the WWVT toolkit website as well as the new Word Vector Interface.

The Pedagogy Team created most of the text for the website, the wireframe for the website, and the assignments and case studies that are found here. They particularly focused on creating a narrative for this tool, and supporting readings and information, so it can be accessed by a wide range of scholars, educators, students, and researchers.
